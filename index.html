About

I've been inside the
strategy function long
enough to know.

I started my career at AstraZeneca as one of 25 graduates recruited globally into their IT Leadership Development Program — and ended up as deputy PM on a $90M digital transformation across 18 global sites. Not because I planned for it. Because someone trusted me with something real, and I didn't want to let it go sideways.

At Bain, I ran strategy engagements across diesel engines, ESG markets, fashion retail, and pharma. I co-authored a white paper on voluntary carbon markets unveiled at Davos, built 5-year plans targeting 2x revenue growth, and validated multi-thousand-crore opportunities across D2C retail and industrial markets.

At Atmus Filtration, where I lead corporate strategy today, I built the market sizing model that became the single source of truth for strategic and sales planning. I've run commercial diligence on acquisition targets and presented findings directly to the CEO.

I've been the person presenting to the CEO. I know what gets challenged, what gets ignored, and what lands. That shapes how I build. I work with a small number of clients at a time — the kind of work where I know the problem well enough to have an opinion, not just a process.

Four problems I solve well

Bespoke engagements. Inquiry-driven. Scoped to what you actually need.

01

Commercial
Due Diligence

You're evaluating an acquisition. The market looks attractive — but you need to know whether the growth story is real, who the competitive set actually is, and whether the customer base is as defensible as the seller says. I've run diligence on industrial filtration targets: market positioning, growth opportunities, risk mapping. I know what a credible thesis looks like — and what a weak one is hiding.

What you get

A thesis you can defend in IC. Clear risks your model needs to price in. Confidence — or a reason to walk away.

PE firms Corp Dev Strategic M&A
02

Market Sizing &
Opportunity Framing

Not the TAM/SAM/SOM slide that everyone ignores. A proprietary model built from first principles — customer segments, adoption curves, realistic capture scenarios, sensitivity cases. The kind that becomes the single source of truth for your planning cycle, not a slide that ages out. At Atmus, I built this model. Elsewhere, I've validated multi-thousand-crore opportunities with surveys of 1,300+ shoppers and bottoms-up financial forecasts.

What you get

A sizing framework you own. A number you can stand behind. The narrative that makes leadership believe it.

03

Growth
Strategy

A 3–6 year growth plan built around your actual constraints — capital, capability, competitive position — not a generic framework dressed up as strategy. Organic and inorganic levers. Financial scenarios that reflect real trade-offs. I've built plans targeting 2x revenue growth for a diesel engine manufacturer, modeled inorganic P&Ls for emerging segments, and led GBS opportunity analyses that went directly to the CEO.

What you get

A plan with a financial backbone. Leadership alignment. A clear answer to "why this, why now."

04

Executive &
Board-Ready Deliverables

The analysis is only as good as the room it survives. I write the deck and the model — structure, narrative, the defensible number, the pre-empted objection. If your output needs to hold up in a board meeting, an investor conversation, or a leadership offsite where someone will push back, I build for that environment specifically.

What you get

A deliverable your team can present with confidence. No last-minute scramble to answer the question no one saw coming.
